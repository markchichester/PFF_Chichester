Letter from the editor

Welcome to the 2026 NFL QB Annual

Two decades ago, a small group of football pioneers set out to build something many thought impossible: a system capable of evaluating individual performance on every snap.

Since then, thousands of people have spent hundreds of thousands of hours watching, grading and charting games. The result is an organization that now serves all 32 NFL teams, 136 FBS programs and almost every major media outlet across the football landscape. Yet despite that growth, the central idea has remained remarkably simple.

PFF has never claimed to be a source of objective football truth, nor was it ever meant to be. Every play contains hundreds of variables. Every evaluation contains context. Every opinion is ultimately shaped by perspective. What PFF has always sought to do is provide information — one piece of a larger and more complicated puzzle.

The grading system, advanced statistics and charting data are not designed to settle debates; they are designed to make them better. They serve as a compass rather than a destination, helping the football world navigate one of the most complex sports in the world with more information than they had before.

That philosophy exists because of the people who built it. Analysts, data scientists, developers, editors and researchers have all contributed to a body of work that changed the way football is discussed, studied and understood. Behind every grade, chart and statistic is an enormous amount of work from people who care deeply about the game.

This annual is the product of that effort.

So it is my pleasure to welcome you to the 2026 edition of the PFF NFL QB Annual — an updated, expanded and long-awaited return of one of our flagship products.

Inside, you'll find comprehensive profiles for every qualifying quarterback from the 2025 season, featuring PFF grades, advanced metrics, situational performance, accuracy data and much more.

Use the leaderboards as your starting point. Explore the profiles. Challenge your assumptions. Draw your own conclusions. Most importantly, enjoy the process.

Thank you for reading, and welcome back to the PFF NFL QB Annual.

Mark Chichester
Head of Editorial · PFF
